Conceptual

Let It Go

Cleaning products represent control and perfectionism— to forcefully extract and remove all things undesirable. Using a formalist approach to still lives of cleaning-related tools, objects are composited with a focus on form, shape, colour, space, or texture in relation to one another. Playing with scale, repetition, and perspectives, 10 separate still life images are placed together. By removing the meaning and purpose of these cleaning tools, and reducing them to their physicality, “Let it Go” hopes to convey a message to weary perfectionists that it’s okay for the less than ideal to exist
